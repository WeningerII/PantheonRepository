Summary

I provided native-language terms for all 133 existing faculties across the four assigned traditions (Kartvelian 5, Ossetian 28, Armenian 53, Mesopotamian 47), plus one appended signature power (Ninsun's divine wisdom, `+divine-wisdom`). Every faculty — including all titles, offices, and rulership concepts — received an attested native term; no em-dashes were used.

Key conventions and findings:
- **Titles/offices are all rendered natively**, never English: Sumerian nam-lugal (kingship), lugal (king), en (priest-ruler); Akkadian šarru / šar Akkadî (king), bēlūtu (dominion), ēntu (high priestess); Armenian nahapet (patriarch), arkʿay (king), tikin (lady); Ossetian ældar (lord), Nart/Nartæ (hero), and clan-eponyms; Georgian mepe-class concepts via attested vocabulary (gmiri "hero," etc.).
- **Inheritability** is `none` for every sphere/office/title. `partial`/`full` is reserved for heritable bodily/material powers: Amirani's deathlessness (full), Batraz's steel body (full), Hayk's bow / David's Lightning-Sword and wonder-horse (partial, as transmitted heirlooms/traits).
- **Cuneiform** is included only where reliably reproducible (theonyms and high-frequency signs: 𒈗 lugal, 𒉆𒈗 nam-lugal, 𒂗 en, 𒂍 é, 𒀭 diĝir/AN, 𒈨 me, 𒁾𒊬 dub-sar, 𒍪𒈨 zà-mí, 𒀭𒈹 Inana). For abstract concepts I led with the standard scholarly transliteration (the load-bearing form per CAD/ETCSL/PSD) rather than risk an incorrect glyph.
- Web-verified the higher-risk terms: Ossetian æxsar/æxsart (valor/courage, Abaev), Nartamongæ (truth-cup), don (water), Nart < *nar; Armenian Višapakʿał (dragon-reaper), ararič, nahapet; Akkadian šar kibrāt arbaʾim / šar kiššati, abāru (lead), bārtu (rebellion), tāḫāzu (battle), ēntu (high priestess); Sumerian níĝ-si-sá (justice), nam-lugal, dub-sar.

One data note on the roster: figure `ossetian_akhsar` lists its Cyrillic alt as "Æхсар" but the field `ossetian-iron-cyrillic` reads "Æхсар" while the romanization says "Æhsar" — I used the linguistically standard æxsar (the valor-word). No changes were made to the input file; output is the formatted blocks above.
