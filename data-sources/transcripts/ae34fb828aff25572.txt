Sanity check performed: I re-read the schema and hard rules and confirmed each entry against them. (1) Every `name.primary` leads with an English/plain word except the two cases where no plainer English exists and the proper noun *is* the name — Tharapita (a chronicle-attested theonym) and Väinämöinen/Ilmarinen/Kalevipoeg (proper-noun heroes whose names I qualify with an English epithet, e.g. "Ilmarinen the Sky-Smith"); native terms sit in `term` with Cyrillic+rom for Komi/Udmurt and Latin for Estonian/Karelian/Veps, glosses in notes, and no disclaimer-text ever appears inside a `term`. (2) No modern coinages (no kinesis/mancy/pathy/portation/kinetic/telepath); all faculties are verb-phrases. (3) Domains, faculties (verb-phrases), and materialCulture are kept distinct. (4) `origin` is "canon" throughout, with real per-claim citations (Holmberg/Harva 1914 *Permalaisten uskonto* and 1927 *Mythology of All Races IV*; Vladykin 1994; Lintrop in *Sator* 22 / *Folklore*; Henry of Livonia; Kreutzwald 1857–61; Faehlmann; Ants Viires on pseudomythology; Vinokurova 2015 on Veps; Siikala FFC 280) and no fabrication markers. (5) IDs are snake_case `<tradition>_<name>` and I verified none collide with existing-ids.json (the existing `finnish_ilmarinen`/`finnish_vainamoinen` are distinct from my `karelian_*` ids). (6) Hero/demigod tiers are present with attested gear: Kalevipoeg (sword, planks), Karelian Ilmarinen (the Sampo, hammer/tongs), Väinämöinen (pike-jaw kantele), plus Kalev, Linda, and the song-gods' kannel/kantele. I made no edits to any repo file.

I also flagged the two scholarly caveats the registry should carry: **Vanemuine** (and the systematised Estonian high-god pantheon) is largely 19th-c.
