**Note on conventions and decisions:**
- **ID conventions followed exactly:** Yoruba and Edo use **bare ids** (e.g. `oshun`, `eshu`, `edo_olokun`) — matching `ogun`/`shango` and `erinmwinde`/`eweka_i`. Wait — Edo existing ids are bare (`erinmwinde`), but those are all *human dynasts*; the *deities* I added carry an `edo_` prefix to disambiguate from the Yoruba `olokun`/`esu` (since `olokun` and `eshu`/`esu` would otherwise collide across traditions in a flat id space). Akan keeps its established `akan_` prefix. Fon and Zulu are new traditions, given `fon_`/`zulu_` prefixes consistent with the file's prefixed-tradition pattern.
- **Skipped (already exist):** Akan Asase Yaa (`akan_asase_yaa`) and Anansi (`akan_anansi`) — not recreated.
- **parentIds** use real attested links within this batch/corpus (e.g. Tano→Nyame+Asase Yaa; Babalu-Aye→Nana Buruku; Oya/Olokun→Yemoja per the lagoon-birth myth; Fon children→Mawu-Lisa→Nana Buluku). Aganju, Nana Buruku, Osanobua, Esu, Fa, and the Zulu primordials left `[]` where parentage is not firmly attested.
- **Cross-tradition duplicates intentional:** Nana Buruku appears as Yoruba (`nana_buruku`) and Fon (`fon_nana_buluku`), and Olokun as Yoruba (`olokun`) and Edo (`edo_olokun`), since both are independently and prominently attested in each tradition — matching the registry's existing practice of recension-specific entries (e.g. `macuxi_insikiran` vs `insikiran`).
