## Summary

I authored **28 figures** across the six absent traditions, balanced as: Haida (5), Tlingit (4), Kwakwaka'wakw (6), Taíno (5), Selk'nam (6), Yámana (2). All validated as syntactically correct JSON with every schema rule passing (legal `type` values only — deity/demigod; no forbidden modern-coinage substrings; `id`/`name` keys in materialCulture, never `item`; per-claim citations on every domain, faculty, materialCulture entry, and figure; snake_case IDs collision-checked against `existing-ids.json`).

Key authorial decisions, all flagged in `notes`:
- **Culture-heroes foregrounded** as required: the three cognate Ravens (Haida Yáahl, Tlingit Yéil, Kwakwaka'wakw U'melth), Kénos, Kwányip, the Yoálox brothers, and Master Carpenter — each carrying their attested deeds (releasing the sun, clamshell/rock-and-leaf humans, stealing fresh water) and gear (canoe, harpoon kit).
- **Names lead in English**, with the attested native term in `term` (correct script/rom) and glosses in notes; `term.value` is `""` wherever no native form was recoverable in the consulted scholarship (e.g., Foam Woman, several faculties) rather than inventing one.
- **Zemi handled as objects**, not figures — placed in materialCulture for Yúcahu (three-pointed trigonolith), Guabancex (spiral-armed), and Boinayel (weeping rain-zemi); coppers in Komokwa's materialCulture.
- **Corrections to the brief's framing**, documented in notes: Foam Woman is the **Raven**-moiety ancestress while Djila'qons/Creek Woman is the **Eagle**-moiety ancestress (the prompt's "Property Woman/Djila'qons" conflates two distinct beings); **Juracán** is the Taíno word for "hurricane," recorded as a phenomenon (not personified as a figure); thinly-attested **Kah-lit** folded into the Tlingit Thunderbird's lightning faculty rather than over-asserted as its own figure; Selk'nam sun/moon recorded with both Gusinde's orthography (Kren/Kre) and the brief's (Kran/Kreeh).
- **Citations anchored to primary + reputable secondary scholarship**: Ramón Pané (c. 1498) for Taíno; Swanton (BAE Bulletins 29 & 39, 1905/1909) for Haida and Tlingit; Boas (1897/1910/1930) for Kwakwaka'wakw; Gusinde (1931/1937) and Anne Chapman (1982) for the Fuegians; plus Bringhurst/Reid and the Haida Gwaii Museum for Haida.

A scratch copy is at `/tmp/myth/figures.json` (outside the repo). No repository files were modified.
