A few authorial decisions worth flagging:
- **Parentage**: Māori primordials Ranginui/Papatūānuku get `parentIds: []`; their nine sons (Tāne, Tūmatauenga, Rongo, Haumia-tiketike, Tāwhirimātea, Tangaroa, Whiro) all point to `["polynesian_ranginui","polynesian_papatuanuku"]`. Hine-nui-te-pō points to `["polynesian_tane"]` (her mother Hine-ahu-one isn't in this batch). Slavic Dazhbog and Svarozhich → `["slavic_svarog"]` (in corpus); Zorya → `["slavic_dazhbog"]` (this batch). Hawaiian Pele/Nāmaka and the four great gods get `[]` since their attested parents (Haumea, Kāne Milohai) aren't authored here. Finnish gods are mostly independent; Väinämöinen (already in corpus) is Ilmatar's son but I did not edit existing entries.
- **Types**: All are `deity` (every figure here is an attested god/goddess). I used `era: "primordial"` for cosmogonic figures (Rangi, Papa, Tāne, Ilmatar, Tuoni, Rod, etc.) and `"mythic"` for the rest, since those were the only two enum values offered.
- **Sourcing**: Polynesian → Grey's *Polynesian Mythology* (1855), Te Ara, plus Beckwith's *Hawaiian Mythology* / Westervelt for Hawaiian; Slavic → Primary Chronicle (980 pantheon), Lay of Igor's Campaign, Helmold, Lives of St. Otto, with Ivanov–Toporov as secondary; Finnish → Kalevala (cited by runo), Agricola's 1551 list, Ganander. Belobog is the one figure I weighted `secondary` (it survives mainly as the reconstructed antithesis of Helmold's Chernobog), which I flag honestly rather than overstate.
- **Faculties vs domains** are kept distinct as required (e.g. Tāne's domain is *forests*, but his faculties are *separating earth and sky*, *fashioning the first woman*, *fetching the knowledge-baskets*; Pele's domain is *volcanoes*, her faculty is *erupting lava* + *shapeshifting*).
- Named **material culture** included where attested: Tāne's three kete, Kūkaʻilimoku image, Pele's Pāoa digging-stick, Hina's kapa beater, Ukko's Ukonvasara, Mielikki's forest keys, Triglav's black oracular horse, Marzanna's straw effigy.
