**Coverage:** All 108 faculties termed (Egyptian 53, Kushite 27, Arabian 28) — none skipped, no em-dashes used. Every kingship/title rendered in the figure's own language: Egyptian `nswt-bjty`, `nsyt`, `ḥqꜣ`-class lordship terms (`nb`, `nṯr`); Kushite Meroitic `qore` for native kingship plus the Egyptian titulary the 25th-Dynasty kings actually inscribed for their Egyptian rule; Arabian `rabb`/`malik`/`amīr`/`sayyid` and attested theonym-epithets.

**Key sourcing notes:**
- Egyptian transliterations follow Faulkner *Concise Dictionary of Middle Egyptian* (CDME), the *Wörterbuch* (Wb), and Wilkinson *Complete Gods and Goddesses*, with primary attestations (Pyramid Texts, Shabaka Stone, Kadesh Poem, Deir el-Bahri/Kawa stelae) cited where the specific deed lives.
- Kushite: native Meroitic is sparsely attested, so for the *Kushite* sphere I used Meroitic `qore` (verified, e.g. Amanirenas "qore … kandake") and the theonym `Amani`; for the *Egyptian* sphere of these same kings I used the Egyptian terms from their own monuments — consistent with the prompt's "Egyptian terms used in Kush."
- Arabian: terms drawn from Ibn al-Kalbi's *Kitāb al-Aṣnām*, Herodotus 3.8 (Alilat→Allāt), the Qurʾān (53:19–22 for *banāt Allāh*), and Nabataean epigraphy (`hbl`, Manawat); etymologies verified (Allāt < *al-ilāhat*; al-ʿUzzā < *ʿizza*; Manāt ~ *maniyya*).

A few faculties admit more than one defensible native term (e.g., oracle = `biꜣyt` "manifestation" vs. `wḏ` "decree"); where so I gave the best-attested single term and noted the alternate inline.
