---

**Coverage:** All 108 existing faculties termed (Egyptian 47, Kushite 30, Arabian 31). No em-dashes were needed — every title/office resolved to an attested native word (e.g. kingship → nswt-bjtj / qore / rabb / malik-adjacent amīr/sayyid; priestly-office → ẖrj-ḥb; tribal-noble → sayyid). All inheritability is "none" since every faculty here is a sphere, office, deed, or title rather than a heritable bodily power.

**Key sourcing notes:**
- Egyptian transliterations follow standard egyptological convention (Erman-Grapow *Wörterbuch* references, Pyramid/Coffin Texts, Shabaka Stone, Kadesh inscriptions, Wilkinson *Complete Gods*).
- Kushite uses the genuinely attested Meroitic royal word **qore** (king) — confirmed in Rilly's work — alongside the Egyptian titulary (nswt-bjtj) the Napatan/25th-Dynasty kings actually inscribed, since Napatan-era royal texts were written in Egyptian. **ktke/kandake** (queen-mother) was not needed as no female Kushite ruler appears in the roster.
- Arabian relies on Ibn al-Kalbī's *Kitāb al-Aṣnām*, Herodotus 3.8 (Alilat = al-Lāt), Qurʾān 53 (al-Najm), and the *Kitāb al-Aghānī* for the poet-heroes, with attested concept-words (istiqsām bi-l-azlām, nasīb, fāris, jūd/karam, qirā al-ḍayf).

I did not append signature powers, prioritizing complete coverage of every existing faculty id as instructed.
