**Summary of research**

I researched all 44 roster figures and recorded only genuinely attested powers/items with real sources, skipping the many purely historical figures who have no attested supernatural feat or distinctive mythic item.

**Recorded (12 figures, 26 records):**
- **Naram-Sin** — strongest case: self-deification (DINGIR determinative), title "God of Akkad," "King of the Four Quarters," the Ekur sacrilege in the Curse of Agade, plus his Victory Stele (horned crown of divinity).
- **Amar-Sin, Šu-Sin, Ibbi-Sin** — deified Ur III kings (divine determinative + state cult per Sallaberger's criteria); Šu-Sin additionally performs the sacred-marriage union with Inanna (Love Song of Šu-Sin).
- **Manishtushu** — Obelisk and diorite statues. **Rimush** — his unprecedented cast-metal self-statue before Enlil.
- **Akhenaten** — sole revealer/intermediary of the Aten and "living on Maat" (Great Hymn to the Aten).
- **Ramesses III** — ritual smiting before the gods and the khepesh sword received from Amun-Re (Medinet Habu).
- **Tudhaliya IV & Suppiluliuma II** — Hittite posthumous deification ("becomes a god," CTH 450); Tudhaliya's sword/lituus (Yazılıkaya), Suppiluliuma's divine cult-image of his father (Südburg).
- **'Antara ibn Shaddad** — warhorse Abjar and sword al-Dhāmī.

**Skipped (32 figures)** as having no attested powers/items: the Egyptian queens/consorts and minor royals (Kheredu-ankh, Ahmose, Mutemwiya, Tuya, Tiy-Merenese, Tyti, Iset Ta-Hemdjert, Setnakhte, Neferure, Sitamun, Tausret, Seti II, Merneptah, Amunherkhepeshef, Khaemwaset C, Pentaweret, Ramesses IV, Ramesses VI, Hen-em-Waset), Kushite queens (Abar, Pebatjma), Mesopotamian non-deified/biographical figures (Ur-Nungal, Geme-Ninlilla, Enmaḫgalanna, Šar-kali-šarri, Meli-Šipak II, Ninurta-apal-Ekur, Ashur-dan I), Levantine Ammurapi (administrative texts only), and Arabian Imru' al-Qais (poisoned-robe is a scholarly-rejected legend) and Hatim al-Tai (generosity is a trait, not a power, with no attested mythic item).

Two honest caveats on sourcing: 'Antara's named horse and sword are most prominent in the later Sīrat ʿAntar popular epic (cited as such) rather than his Muʿallaqa proper; and several cuneiform glyph strings above are standard normalized spellings (DINGIR, Ekur, Inanna, Enlil) rather than sign-for-sign copies of a specific tablet.
