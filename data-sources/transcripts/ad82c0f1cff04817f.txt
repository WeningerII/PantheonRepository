## Research summary

**Buganda** (Roscoe, *The Baganda*, 1911): Katonda is the supreme creator; the *Balubaale* are a pantheon (Roscoe counts ~73) of deified heroes/nature-powers served by mediums at temples. Mukasa (Lake Nalubaale/Victoria, health, fertility, harvest; temple on Bubembe Island) and his younger brother Kibuka (war god, slain after taking a captured Banyoro woman; shrine at Mbaale) are sons of Wanema; Musisi (earthquakes) is their forebear. Kawumpuli = plague. In the Kintu cycle the sky-father is Ggulu, whose daughter Nambi married Kintu (first man → demigod/scion), and whose son Walumbe (death) followed them to earth.

**Bunyoro** (Beattie, *The Nyoro State* / *Bunyoro: An African Kingdom*): Ruhanga the remote creator/sky-god heads the Batembuzi; Nyamiyonga rules the underworld of the dead and lured King Isaza below. The Bacwezi are a semi-divine dynasty — Ndahura (founder) and Wamara (last ruler) — who vanished and are venerated through the Kubandwa possession cult → demigod tier.

**Dinka** (Lienhardt, *Divinity and Experience*, 1961): Nhialic = supreme Divinity/sky. Free-divinities: Deng (rain/sky), Garang (associated with men, healing/misfortune; with Abuk the first pair), Abuk (first woman, patroness of women and gardens, snake emblem), and Macardit (the "fierce" divinity of sterility/death's bitter end).

**Nuer** (Evans-Pritchard, *Nuer Religion*, 1956): Kwoth (Spirit/God), refracted into spirits of the above — Deng (sky-spirit, sickness), Buk (Deng's mother), Col — and the *colwic*, souls of those killed by lightning who become spirits of the above.

**Kongo** (MacGaffey, e.g. *Religion and Society in Central Africa*): Nzambi a Mpungu, supreme creator; Nzambici, female counterpart; the *bisimbi/simbi*, local water/earth nature-spirits and the *bakulu* (dead) across the *kalunga* line dividing Nseke (living) from Mpemba (dead).

**Schema/convention notes:**
- All ids follow `<tradition>_<name>` snake_case; verified against `data-sources/existing-ids.json` — no collisions.
- `sphereId` values use the registry's kebab-case descriptive vocabulary (confirmed in `data-sources/transcripts`, e.g. `creation`, `death`, `underworld`, `war`, `rain-sky`).
- `type` uses the attested set (`deity`, `demigod`, `spirit`); the Bacwezi (Ndahura, Wamara) and the Buganda first humans (Kintu, Nambi) are `demigod`, matching the requested culture-hero tier.
- All `origin` set to `"canon"`; every domain, faculty, and figure-level `sources[]` carries a real ethnographic citation (Roscoe, Beattie, Lienhardt, Evans-Pritchard, MacGaffey). Native terms are given in Luganda/Runyoro/Dinka/Nuer/Kikongo with romanization; unrecorded terms are `""` (never invented, never disclaimed).

**Two scholarly judgment calls worth flagging:** (1) In Roscoe's Kintu cycle the sky-father is **Ggulu**, distinct from the supreme creator **Katonda** of the broader Lubaale system — I included both and recorded Kintu's parents as empty (his origin is unstated) while Nambi/Walumbe descend from Ggulu. (2) The **colwic** are a class of spirits rather than a single named figure; I modeled them as one collective `spirit` entry with `sex:"unknown"`, consistent with Evans-Pritchard's treatment.
