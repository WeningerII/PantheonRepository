**Summary of research (23 figures researched; 14 recorded, 9 skipped as having no attested personal power/item):**

- **Kartvelian (Amirani epic, Svan tradition):** Recorded all four. **Sulkalmakhi** (foster-father) cuts the living Amirani from slain Dali's womb and raises him; **Darejan** (jealous wife) slays the goddess Dali by shearing her hair; **Badri** and **Usup** (foster-brothers) fight devs/the three-headed giant and aid Amirani's dragon-belly escape. Many feats are shared among the brothers (attested as such).
- **Ossetian (Nart sagas):** **Akhsartæg** and **Uryzmæg** and **Khæmyts** are richly attested. Akhsartæg shoots the apple-thieving dove, tracks it by blood, dives to Donbettyr, weds Dzerassa. Uryzmæg leads the Narts, blinds the one-eyed giant and escapes under its flock (the Ossetian "Cyclops" tale), leads raids. Khæmyts owns the love-compelling **Tooth of Arkyz** (Аркъызы дандаг) and an extraordinary sword (æхсаргард), is near-invulnerable except his intestines, carries his shape-shifting frog-wife Bytsenon, and bears Batraz from a tumor on his back. **Akhsar** has only thin shared feats (guarding the apple tree, the pursuit) — recorded conservatively. **Wærxæg SKIPPED** — pure progenitor/genealogy with no personal feat or personally-wielded item.
- **Armenian (Movses Khorenatsi; Daredevils of Sassoun):** **Aram** recorded (defeats Medes/Nyukar Madēs, giant Barsham, Titan Payapis Kaalea; expands the realm). **Anushavan Sosanver** recorded (divines from the rustling plane-tree grove at Armavir). **Baghdasar** recorded (with Sanasar defeats the caliph's army, co-founds Sassoun). **SKIPPED: Aramaneak** and **Aramayis** (only eponymous settlement/founding, no ability), **Ara the Beautiful** (passive — refuses Semiramis and is slain; the aralez resurrection is done to him), **Misra Melik** (defeated antagonist; only demands tribute, no positive feat).
- **Finnish (Kalevala):** **Aino** recorded (transforms into a salmon/lohi and escapes Väinämöinen, Runo IV–V).
- **Polynesian (Grey):** **Makeatutara** recorded (recites the dedicatory karakia over Māui, botching it). **Taranga** recorded (descends daily to the underworld through a tunnel; wraps Māui in her topknot/tikitiki). **SKIPPED: Kaitangata** (deceived and abandoned by Whaitiri), **Hemā** (captured/blinded victim of the Ponaturi), **Wahieroa** (killed by Matuku — passive victim).

Note on native-term caveats: Some Ossetic terms (e.g., the descriptive phrases like "fosy byn airvæzyn") are constructed from attested Iron vocabulary describing the attested deed rather than quoted proper nouns; the proper-noun items (Аркъызы дандаг, Нарты фæткъуы, æхсаргард, Доныбеттыр) are genuinely attested. The Māori "ki te rua o te ao" similarly renders the attested act (descent to the underworld) in the language rather than a fixed epithet. Where variant attributions exist (notably Darejan/Sulkalmakhi roles in the Amirani birth), I followed the standard Svan tradition and cited it.
