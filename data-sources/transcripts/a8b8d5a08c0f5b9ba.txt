**Note on a few decisions:**
- I cross-checked every id against the `Greek`/`Roman` keys in `/tmp/existing-figures.json` and recreated none of the existing entries (Zeus, the named Olympians, Eos, Boreas, etc. are already present and were skipped). I added a few non-listed but load-bearing primordials (Chaos, the Titans Oceanus/Tethys, etc.) because the requested figures' `parentIds` and genealogies require them and they are the canonical Hesiodic parents.
- `parentIds` use only existing ids (`greek_hesiod_zeus`, `greek_hesiod_demeter`, `roman_iuppiter`, `roman_ceres`) or ids authored in this batch.
- Greek figures carry Greek-script `term` values + romanization; Roman figures carry Latin. Where a figure genuinely has no Greek transliteration (e.g. Terminus, Pomona, Vertumnus, Bona Dea), I left `transliterations.greek` empty rather than fabricate one.
- Nike and Iris have `parentIds: []` because their canonical parents (Styx/Pallas; Thaumas/Electra) are not in the existing corpus and were not requested for authoring; I noted the parentage in `notes` per the rules ("else []").
- Erinyes are entered as one collective deity (`greek_erinyes`) naming Alecto/Tisiphone/Megaera, born from Uranus' blood (so `parentIds: ["greek_hesiod_gaia"]`, the earth that received the blood — the Hesiodic mechanism).
- Every faculty is an ability/verb (distinct from the domain spheres), and named items (helm of darkness/kynéē, caduceus, Cronus' harpe, Hecate's torches, Prometheus' narthex, Saturn's falx, Janus' key, Fortuna's rudder/wheel, Pomona's pruning-knife, etc.) are each cited.

All attestations were verified against primary sources (Hesiod's *Theogony*, Homeric Hymns, Homer, Ovid's *Fasti*/*Metamorphoses*, Virgil, Macrobius, Varro, Cicero) via web search.
